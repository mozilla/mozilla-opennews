Building a news ecosystem on the open web

What is OpenNews? Knight-Mozilla OpenNews is about creating a diverse ecosystem to help journalism thrive on the open web. It's about producing open source code that solves real problems in news. It's about supporting communities of developers and journalists as they make, learn, and invent together. And it's about deploying fellows—and code—into news organizations to collaborate in new ways.

Knight-Mozilla OpenNews is the evolution of the Knight-Mozilla News Technology Partnership. Here's what's on tap in 2012:

Fellowships

Our new class of 2012/13 Knight-Mozilla Fellows will be embedded in partner newsrooms around the world to spend a year solving real-world journalistic problems next to reporters and news apps developers. Fellows work in the open, sharing their code and their discoveries with the larger development community.

Making

This year we'll be sponsoring or organizing fifteen hackfests to write code that helps to solve real-world journalistic problems. From Africa to America, we're looking for partners to hack the news with us!

Learning

Go back to school with our 2012 online learning labs, launching this spring, that offer seminars, tutorials, and full-fledged classes that offer new skills to both developers and journalists.

Community

The hacker-journalist community is a vibrant one, and through OpenNews we're hoping to grow its size through these initiatives as well as through a new site, Source, that will debut later this year that highlights the best code (and coders) of the community.

About Knight

The John S. and James L. Knight Foundation advances journalism in the digital age and invests in the vitality of communities where the Knight brothers owned newspapers.

Knight Foundation focuses on projects that promote informed and engaged communities and lead to transformational change.

For more, visit www.knightfoundation.org

About Mozilla

Mozilla is a global non-profit dedicated to putting you in control of your online experience and shaping the future of the web for the public good.

We do this by creating great software, like the Firefox browser, and building communities, like Mozilla Drumbeat, that build and grow the open web
